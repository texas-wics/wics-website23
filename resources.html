Research Guide

  • Sign up for the directed reading program (DiRP)
  • Look up professors who do research in areas you are interested in
    • Take their classes (not required)
    • Read their papers (strongly recommend) - will help you to show that you are aware of what kind of research they do
    • Email them to ask about doing research with them
  • Research options
    • Course credit by doing a CS370
    • URA position which is often paid (ranges between 10-20/hr week)
  • If a professor's research interests you and they are not in the CS department, you can still reach out to them
  • Research experiences for undergraduates

Grad School Guide

  • Masters
    • 2 years
    • UT offers a 5 year BS + MS program you can apply to your junior spring
    • Have to pay to attend
  • PhD
    • ~6 years
    • Very research oriented
    • You DON’T need a masters to do a PhD
    • Will be paid to attend (not a lot of money though)
  • Grad school application requires writing a Statement of Purpose (SoP)
  • GRE
    • A standardized test that you will need to take before October of your senior year
    • Many schools require you to submit a GRE result
    • There are also quite a few schools that leave the GRE as optional
  • You need recommendation letters from professors
    • Build connections with professors
    • Talk to them and learn about their journeys, ask for advice
